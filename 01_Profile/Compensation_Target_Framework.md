# Compensation Target Framework v1.0

## Purpose

Define the compensation strategy for Julian Cabrera Marceglia's international career transition.

The objective is not to maximize salary alone.

The objective is to maximize:

* Total compensation.
* Career acceleration.
* International market positioning.
* Long-term earning potential.

---

Compensation decisions must consider:

```text
Offer Value =

Base Compensation
+
Variable Compensation
+
Benefits
+
Relocation Support
+
Career Growth

-

Transition Cost
```

# Compensation Evaluation Factors

Every offer should evaluate:

## Financial Factors

* Base salary.
* Bonus.
* Equity when applicable.
* Benefits.
* Relocation package.
* Housing support.
* Visa assistance.

---

## Career Factors

* Role seniority.
* Technical exposure.
* International visibility.
* Future market value.
* Learning acceleration.

---

## Personal Transition Factors

* Country.
* Cost of living.
* Tax impact.
* Relocation complexity.
* Family impact.
* Travel requirements.

---

# Relocation Principle

Permanent relocation is acceptable when:

The total opportunity value justifies leaving the current location.

Relocation is not rejected.

Relocation is not free.

Any permanent move must compensate for:

* disruption cost;
* adaptation effort;
* opportunity cost;
* personal transition.

---

# Offer Classification

## Strategic Offer

Characteristics:

* Strong role alignment.
* Significant salary improvement.
* International career acceleration.
* Builds future market value.

Action:

Prioritize.

---

## Acceptable Offer

Characteristics:

* Good compensation.
* Reasonable career growth.
* Strong company.

Action:

Evaluate carefully.

---

## Low Strategic Value Offer

Characteristics:

* Similar compensation.
* Limited growth.
* Repeats current positioning.

Action:

Usually reject.

---

# Negotiation Principles

Never negotiate only from current salary.

The negotiation argument should be based on:

* 21+ years manufacturing experience.
* Automotive production environment knowledge.
* Digital manufacturing capabilities.
* Industrial data platform development.
* Ability to connect factory operations and software systems.

---

# Market Feedback Usage

Compensation discussions provide market information.

Track:

* Salary ranges offered.
* Countries valuing the profile.
* Role levels assigned.
* Recruiter perception.

Use this information to refine targeting.

---

# Decision Rule

A lower salary may be acceptable only if:

* international positioning improves significantly;
* future earning potential increases;
* the role opens a stronger market segment.

A higher salary may be rejected if:

* role reduces positioning;
* career trajectory decreases;
* experience is underutilized.
