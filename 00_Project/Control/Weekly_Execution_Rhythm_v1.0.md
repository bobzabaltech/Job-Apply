# Weekly Execution Rhythm v1.0

## Purpose

Define the weekly operating cadence for Julian Cabrera Marceglia's international career transition project.

The objective is to maintain consistent market exposure, generate feedback, and continuously improve positioning.

---

# Operating Principle

Career transition is treated as a market validation process.

Every week must generate:

* new market exposure;
* new conversations;
* new information;
* strategic improvements.

---

# Weekly Cycle

## Monday — Market Intelligence

Activities:

* Review new target roles.
* Identify relevant companies.
* Analyze market terminology.
* Update opportunity priorities.

Output:

New opportunities added to pipeline.

---

## Tuesday / Wednesday — Applications

Activities:

* Select highest-value opportunities.
* Customize CV.
* Prepare application message.
* Submit applications.

Target:

High-quality applications.

---

## Thursday — Networking

Activities:

* Recruiter outreach.
* Hiring manager connections.
* Professional conversations.

Target:

Create opportunities beyond job portals.

---

## Friday — Review

Analyze:

* Applications sent.
* Responses.
* Conversations.
* Market feedback.

Update:

* targeting;
* messaging;
* priorities.

---

# Minimum Weekly Activity

Initial validation phase:

## Applications

5-10 highly aligned applications.

## Networking

5-10 targeted contacts.

## Market Research

Continuous improvement of company and role intelligence.

---

# Quality Rule

Never increase volume by reducing alignment.

A poor-fit application provides little learning.

A well-targeted application provides:

* market signal;
* recruiter feedback;
* interview opportunity.

---

# Weekly Review Questions

## Market

What roles are appearing repeatedly?

---

## Positioning

How is the market interpreting the profile?

---

## Conversion

Where is the funnel breaking?

* discovery;
* response;
* interview;
* offer.

---

## Strategy

What should increase?

What should stop?

What should be tested?
