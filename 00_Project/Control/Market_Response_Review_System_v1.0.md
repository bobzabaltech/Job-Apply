# Market Response Review System v1.0

## Purpose

Define the process for analyzing external market feedback during Julian Cabrera Marceglia's international job search.

The objective is to convert market interactions into strategic improvements.

---

# Operating Principle

The market provides data.

Every:

* application;
* recruiter conversation;
* interview;
* rejection;
* salary discussion;

is a signal that helps optimize positioning.

---

# Weekly Review Cycle

Frequency:

Weekly.

Inputs:

* Applications submitted.
* Recruiter responses.
* Interview outcomes.
* Job descriptions analyzed.
* Market terminology observed.

---

# Funnel Metrics

## Awareness

Measure:

* Recruiter views.
* LinkedIn profile visits.
* Connection acceptance.

Purpose:

Evaluate market visibility.

---

## Interest

Measure:

* Replies.
* Recruiter conversations.
* Requests for additional information.

Purpose:

Evaluate positioning effectiveness.

---

## Conversion

Measure:

* Screening interviews.
* Technical interviews.
* Hiring manager interviews.
* Offers.

Purpose:

Evaluate opportunity quality.

---

# Analysis Categories

## Positioning Signal

Question:

How is the market interpreting the profile?

Possible outcomes:

Positive:

"Manufacturing Systems"

"Digital Manufacturing"

"Industrial Data"

Negative:

"Quality Technician"

"Metrology only"

Action:

Adjust narrative emphasis.

---

## Role Signal

Track:

Which titles generate interest?

Examples:

* Manufacturing Systems Engineer.
* Digital Manufacturing Engineer.
* MES Engineer.
* Industrial Data Engineer.

Action:

Increase focus on high-performing role families.

---

## Geographic Signal

Track:

* Countries generating responses.
* Salary ranges.
* Relocation discussions.

Action:

Update geographic priorities.

---

## Skill Signal

Track recurring requirements:

Examples:

* MES.
* SQL.
* Python.
* PLC.
* Cloud.
* Automation.
* Data visualization.

Action:

Separate:

Already supported skills.

Skills requiring strengthening.

---

# Decision Rules

## Low Response Rate

Possible causes:

* Wrong target roles.
* Weak keywords.
* Insufficient customization.
* LinkedIn positioning issue.

Do not immediately conclude lack of market value.

---

## Recruiter Interest but No Interviews

Possible causes:

* Role mismatch.
* Compensation mismatch.
* Narrative clarity.

---

## Interviews but No Offers

Possible causes:

* Interview storytelling.
* Technical depth.
* Seniority positioning.
* Negotiation.

---

# Weekly Output

Every review should produce:

## Keep

Strategies that work.

## Change

Elements with weak performance.

## Test

New hypotheses.
