# Opportunity Evaluation Framework v1.0

## Purpose

Define the decision framework used to evaluate international job opportunities before investing time in CV customization, applications or interview preparation.

The objective is to maximize long-term compensation and career value while minimizing effort spent on low-return opportunities.

This framework is used together with:

* Job_Application_Execution_Workflow_v1.0.md
* Initial_Target_Company_Pipeline_v1.0.md
* Market Research documentation

---

# Core Principle

Not every opportunity deserves an application.

Time is the scarcest resource in this project.

Every application competes with every other opportunity for that time.

Applications should therefore be treated as investment decisions.

---

# Evaluation Process

```text
Opportunity Identified

        ↓

Eligibility Check

        ↓

Strategic Fit

        ↓

Business Value

        ↓

Opportunity Score

        ↓

Priority Assignment

        ↓

GO / NO-GO Decision
```

---

# Stage 1 — Eligibility Check

Reject immediately if any of the following apply:

* Compensation is clearly below target.
* Role is unrelated to Manufacturing Systems, Industrial Data, Manufacturing Intelligence or Digital Manufacturing.
* Position represents a clear career regression.
* Employer has an unacceptable reputation.
* Mandatory requirements cannot realistically be met.

If the opportunity passes these checks, continue with scoring.

---

# Stage 2 — Evaluation Criteria

## 1. Strategic Alignment (30%)

Question:

Does the role strengthen the long-term positioning?

Highest score:

* Manufacturing Systems
* Digital Manufacturing
* Manufacturing Intelligence
* Industrial Data Platforms

Lower score:

* Traditional Quality
* Pure Production Supervision
* Generic Mechanical Engineering

---

## 2. Compensation Potential (25%)

Evaluate:

* Base salary.
* Bonus.
* Equity.
* Relocation support.
* Visa sponsorship.
* Benefits.
* Long-term earning potential.

Compensation should be evaluated over total value, not salary alone.

---

## 3. Growth Potential (15%)

Consider:

* International exposure.
* Technical complexity.
* Leadership opportunities.
* Learning potential.
* Future market value.

---

## 4. Company Quality (10%)

Evaluate:

* Engineering reputation.
* Manufacturing maturity.
* Digital transformation initiatives.
* Financial stability.
* Innovation culture.

Preference is given to organizations where digital manufacturing is a strategic capability rather than an isolated project.

---

## 5. Geographic Value (10%)

Evaluate:

* Country.
* City.
* Relocation attractiveness.
* Cost of living.
* Long-term career ecosystem.

Project policy:

Permanent relocation is acceptable whenever total compensation justifies it.

---

## 6. Application Effort (10%)

Estimate:

* Required customization.
* Required documentation.
* Assessment complexity.
* Expected interview process.

Lower effort for equal expected value increases priority.

# Relocation Policy

The project does not optimize for remote, hybrid or on-site work.

The governing principle is:

> Work where the opportunity creates the greatest long-term value.

Relocation should be evaluated economically rather than emotionally.

Higher compensation, stronger career acceleration and greater strategic value justify permanent relocation.

---

# Company Evaluation Questions

Before applying, answer:

* Does this company invest in Digital Manufacturing?
* Does this company value industrial data?
* Will this role increase future market value?
* Will this position strengthen the professional narrative?
* Can one or both case studies be presented naturally during interviews?
* Would accepting this role make the next career move easier?

The more positive answers, the higher the opportunity quality.

---

# Market Intelligence Feedback

If a job description introduces:

* new recurring keywords;
* emerging technologies;
* new role titles;
* changing recruiter expectations;

record the observation in the Market Research section of the Career_Package.

Applications are also research activities.

---

# Decision Principles

When two opportunities compete for the same effort:

Choose the one with the higher expected long-term return.

Do not optimize for:

* application count;
* interview count;
* recruiter response count.

Optimize for:

* expected compensation;
* positioning;
* market value;
* long-term career acceleration.

---

# Review Cycle

Review this framework whenever:

* a significant market trend emerges;
* recruiter feedback changes consistently;
* interview patterns reveal new positioning opportunities;
* long-term project objectives evolve.

The framework should evolve based on evidence rather than assumptions.

---

# Final Rule

The objective is not to obtain the next job.

The objective is to build an international career with the highest achievable long-term market value.

Every opportunity should be evaluated against that objective before any time is invested.
