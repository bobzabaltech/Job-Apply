# CV Positioning Guidelines 2026

## Purpose

This document defines the positioning guidelines for all CV versions created within Career_Package.

Its objective is to ensure that professional experience, technical capabilities, portfolio projects, and manufacturing background are consistently translated into the language used by the international market.

This document provides operational guidance for:

* CV updates;
* LinkedIn optimization;
* job-specific tailoring;
* application messaging;
* portfolio alignment.

It does not replace strategic decisions documented in:

`00_Project/Strategic_Decisions.md`

Instead, it defines how those decisions should be reflected in professional communication assets.

---

# 1. Core Positioning Principle

The candidate should not be positioned as a generic software or data professional.

The strongest market advantage comes from the combination of:

**Manufacturing Expertise + Software Engineering + Industrial Data**

The CV must communicate that the candidate is a manufacturing technology professional capable of designing and implementing digital solutions for industrial environments.

The primary market identity is:

**Manufacturing Digitalization Engineer**

with supporting identities:

* Industrial Software Engineer
* Manufacturing Systems Engineer
* Industrial Data Engineer
* Smart Manufacturing Specialist

---

# 3. Experience Translation Rules

## Rule 1 — Lead With Business Impact

Avoid presenting activities only.

Weak:

"Developed Python scripts for measurement data processing."

Strong:

"Developed industrial data solutions that transformed manufacturing measurements into actionable production intelligence."

The first describes a task.

The second describes a digital manufacturing capability.

---

## Rule 2 — Translate Manufacturing Experience Into Digital Value

Manufacturing knowledge should always connect to technology impact.

Examples:

Instead of:

"Managed quality inspection processes."

Use:

"Leveraged manufacturing quality expertise to design data-driven systems improving production visibility and decision making."

---

## Rule 3 — Avoid Narrow Technical Labels

Avoid positioning experience around overly specific tools or processes.

Examples:

Lower-value framing:

* Metrology specialist
* Quality engineer
* SPC analyst
* Measurement technician

Higher-value framing:

* Manufacturing systems specialist
* Industrial data professional
* Digital manufacturing engineer
* Manufacturing intelligence engineer

Specific expertise can remain in achievements, but should not define the professional identity.

---

# 4. Headline Guidelines

The headline should maximize alignment with target searches.

Preferred structure:

`Target Role | Technical Domain | Industrial Differentiator`

Examples:

## Recommended

Manufacturing Digitalization Engineer | Industrial Data Platforms | Smart Manufacturing

Industrial Data & Manufacturing Systems Engineer | Software | Quality Analytics

Manufacturing Software Engineer | Digital Factory Systems | Industrial Data

---

## Avoid

Data Engineer | Python | SQL

Reason:

This removes the manufacturing advantage and increases competition.

---

# 5. Professional Summary Guidelines

The summary should follow this order:

1. Manufacturing experience.
2. Digital transformation capability.
3. Software and data engineering skills.
4. Industrial impact.

Recommended narrative:

"Manufacturing technology professional combining extensive automotive production experience with software engineering and industrial data capabilities to build digital systems that improve operational visibility, quality, and decision making."

# 10. Validation Checklist

Before publishing any CV version verify:

## Positioning

* Does the first impression communicate manufacturing digitalization?
* Is software/data presented as an industrial capability?
* Is manufacturing expertise positioned as a competitive advantage?

## Content

* Are achievements described by impact?
* Are portfolio projects mapped to target roles?
* Are market keywords naturally included?

## Consistency

* Does the CV align with LinkedIn?
* Does it support the current Strategic Decisions?
* Does it reinforce the target role strategy?

---

# 11. Strategic Principle

The candidate should compete where manufacturing experience increases value.

The objective is not to appear as another software or data candidate.

The objective is to become a rare profile combining:

**Deep Manufacturing Knowledge + Modern Digital Engineering Capability**

This positioning maximizes differentiation, interview probability, and compensation potential.
