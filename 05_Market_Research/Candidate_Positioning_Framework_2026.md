# Candidate Positioning Framework 2026

## Purpose

This document defines the central professional positioning strategy for the candidate.

Its purpose is to create a consistent narrative across:

* CV;
* LinkedIn profile;
* application messages;
* recruiter conversations;
* interviews;
* portfolio presentation.

The positioning is derived from:

* LinkedIn market analysis;
* target roles;
* employer intelligence;
* candidate experience;
* industrial case studies.

---

# Strategic Objective

The objective is not to maximize the number of possible job matches.

The objective is to maximize market value by positioning the candidate where the combination of experience and capabilities creates the strongest differentiation.

---

Many candidates have:

* analytics skills.

The candidate combines both domains.

> Understanding industrial problems from real manufacturing environments and creating digital systems that improve operational decisions.

# Positioning Rules

## Always emphasize

* manufacturing domain expertise;
* industrial problem solving;
* digital transformation;
* measurable operational impact.

---

## Avoid emphasizing alone

* programming languages;
* generic software development;
* generic analytics.

Technical skills support the story.

They are not the story.
