About the job

About The Role

At ARX Robotics, the quality and traceability of manufacturing data is mission-critical. As Manufacturing Digitalization Specialist, you will take ownership of capturing, structuring, and digitalizing all relevant data generated across our production environment. From robotic platform configurations and assembly stages to component tracking and build status visibility.

You act as the critical link between the shop floor and our SAP S/4HANA system, translating physical production realities into clean, reliable, and actionable data. This role sits at the intersection of production operations and IT, and requires someone equally comfortable in both worlds: familiar enough with manufacturing to understand what happens on the floor, and technically capable of bringing that information into structured, scalable digital processes.

As ARX Robotics scales production of its autonomous ground systems, you will own the data foundation that makes it possible.

What You'll Build

    Capture and digitalize production data across all manufacturing stages, including platform build status, component usage (e.g. parts, screws, subassemblies), and robot system configurations
    Maintain and continuously improve production-related data structures in SAP S/4HANA, including production orders, routings, work centers, and material movements
    Ensure full traceability of each unit across all production stages, from initial assembly through to final system integration and handover
    Systematically collect and consolidate shop floor data to feed continuous improvement processes, identifying patterns, deviations, and optimization potential across production workflows
    Ensure that relevant digital information is available directly at the workstation, enabling operators to work efficiently and with full context
    Collaborate closely with Production, Engineering, and Quality to identify data gaps, define data capture standards, and ensure process-aligned documentation
    Support the setup and optimization of digital workflows that replace manual or paper-based data collection on the shop floor
    Monitor data quality and completeness across connected systems, proactively flagging inconsistencies, and serve as a go-to contact for SAP-related questions
    Contribute to the development of production dashboards and KPIs (e.g. via Power BI or similar tools) to make manufacturing progress and bottlenecks visible in real time

What You Bring

    Fluency in English is required, as well as basic conversational German (B1 or above preferred)
    Completed degree or apprenticeship in industrial engineering, business informatics, mechatronics, production technology, or a comparable field
    Several years of professional experience in a production or manufacturing environment, with hands-on exposure to operational data capture and process documentation
    Working knowledge of SAP sufficient to maintain data, trigger transactions, and support process integration; prior SAP certification is not required
    Strong IT affinity and the ability to quickly learn new systems, tools, and digital workflows
    Structured and detail-oriented working style with a genuine interest in making complex production processes transparent and traceable
    Proactive personality who is comfortable spending time on the shop floor and translating observations into well-structured data
    Good communication skills with the ability to collaborate across technical and non-technical teams

Please note: You do not need to meet every single requirement to apply. We welcome motivated candidates who are eager to grow into the role and develop their expertise further.

What We Offer

Join a mission-driven European defense tech company at a pivotal stage of growth, where you can help shape meaningful technology, take ownership early, and grow with the business

    27 vacation days to start, increasing with tenure up to 30 days

    Learning & development support and team events budget
    Employee discounts and flexible benefits budget via Probonio
    Hybrid working setup depending on role and team needs
    Structured salary bands, levelling framework, and bi-annual development conversations
    Referral bonus program, dog-friendly office, and relocation support where applicable

About Us

ARX is a dual-use technology company that specializes in developing autonomous unmanned systems and sensors for the European defence sector. Our dual-use unmanned ground systems and sensors assist NATO Armies and civilian first responders in tackling their most challenging problems by providing them with training and information to make better decisions and take effective actions in life-or-death situations.

As our team continues to expand rapidly, we are seeking motivated candidates who are excited to work in a highly energetic, creative, and innovative team dedicated to developing autonomous systems for challenging environments. We operate in a dynamic sector where collaboration, sharing innovative ideas, and developing novel problem-solving strategies are ingrained in our work culture.

If you are passionate about overcoming challenges that have a real impact on the security of our democracies, apply here to join ARX and help shape the future of dual-use technology!

ARX is an equal-opportunity employer, and we encourage candidates from all backgrounds to apply.

Actualmente, estamos buscando un/a Ingeniero/a de Digitalización Industrial (Industrial AI) para incorporarse a uno de nuestros proyectos en Zaragoza, dando soporte a entornos del sector automoción.

Tus Principales Funciones Incluirán

    Soporte en la ejecución de la estrategia de digitalización industrial a nivel planta/región.
    Participación en proyectos de Industrial AI aplicados a eficiencia productiva, mantenimiento predictivo y control de calidad.

    Conocimientos de sistemas de gestión de calidad (ISO 9001).
    Conocimientos en análisis de datos (valorable Python, herramientas de data science o IA).
    Nivel alto de inglés.
    Actitud proactiva, capacidad de trabajo en equipo y orientación a mejora continua.

Muy Valorable

About the job

Location: Barcelona, Spain

Thales people architect identity management and data protection solutions at the heart of digital security. Business and governments rely on us to bring trust to the billons of digital interactions they have with people. Our technologies and services help banks exchange funds, people cross borders, energy become smarter and much more. More than 30,000 organizations already rely on us to verify the identities of people and things, grant access to digital services, analyze vast quantities of information and encrypt data to make the connected world more secure.

Thales in Spain is a leader in technological solutions applied to Defence, Aeronautics, Security, Transportation and Space and, furthermore, is a global centre for excellence in Space, Security of Critical Infrastructures and Transportation. With a turnover of €320 million and a staff of 1,200, it exports approximately 40% of its total production principally to the Middle East, North Africa and Latin America.

Thales Cybersecurity & Digital Identity es un líder global en seguridad digital, aportando confianza a un mundo cada vez más conectado. Diseñamos y ofrecemos una amplia gama de productos, software y servicios basados en dos tecnologías principales: la identificación digital y la protección de datos.

Debido a un aumento en la actividad, estamos buscando un Ingeniero de Digitalización, con sede en Parets del Vallès, Barcelona. Como parte del Equipo de Digitalización, serás responsable de:

    Ejecutar iniciativas de digitalización industrial e Industry 4.0 en procesos de producción, ingeniería, calidad y áreas de soporte.
    Implementar y dar soporte técnico a soluciones digitales de manufactura (MES, rastreabilidad, digital workflows, captura automática de datos).
    Garantizar la integración de datos entre sistemas industriales, asegurando consistencia, trazabilidad y disponibilidad de información operativa.
    Soportar la digitalización de procesos de NPI, asegurando que nuevos productos y procesos cuenten con validaciones digitales, rastreabilidad y datos disponibles desde el lanzamiento.
    Implementar y mantener dashboards e indicadores digitales para el seguimiento de desempeño operativo y de calidad.
    Apoyar la digitalización operativa del QMS, asegurando que las herramientas digitales soporten registro, evidencias, auditorías y cumplimiento normativo a nivel de ejecución.
    Ejecutar automatizaciones ligeras y mejoras digitales que reduzcan tareas manuales, errores operativos y retrabajos.
    Colaborar estrechamente con Producción, Ingeniería, Calidad, NPI, Mantenimiento e IT para asegurar la correcta adopción de las soluciones digitales.
    Capacitar y dar soporte a usuarios finales en el uso de herramientas digitales implantadas.

About the job

The opportunity

We are now looking for a Digitalization and Automation Factory Specialist/Lead at Hitachi Energy, Transformers business in Vaasa. The position is fixed-term for the period September 1, 2026 – April 30, 2027.

As part of the factory operations team, you will be responsible for the integration of digital technologies and automation within a manufacturing environment. Your responsibilities will include implementing smart factory initiatives, optimizing production processes through automation, and ensuring seamless connectivity of digital systems. You will support and coordinate digital and automation projects in line with HUB’s digital and automation strategy.

How You’ll Make An Impact

    Develop and implement strategies for the integration of digital technologies and automation in the manufacturing process.
    Oversee the deployment and integration of Industry 4.0 technologies within the factory, including IoT devices, robotics, and other automation solutions.
    Analyse existing production workflows and identify opportunities for automation to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance overall productivity.
    Implement systems for monitoring key performance indicators related to digital and automation processes, ensuring continuous improvement and adherence to production goals.
    Improve and further develop digital reports, digital KPI dashboards, (digital) visual reporting, data analysis and digital inspection cards (QIC). 
    Collaborate with different internal stakeholders to ensure seamless integration of digital and automation solutions, fostering a cohesive and interconnected manufacturing environment.
    Lead initiatives to transform the factory into a smart facility, utilizing real-time data and analytics to make informed decisions and optimize operations.
    Ensure that digital and automation solutions contribute to maintaining or improving product quality standards, implementing measures to address any deviations.
    Provide training programs for factory personnel on new technologies, automation systems, and digital processes to ensure effective implementation and operation.
    Identify and address potential risks associated with digital and automation technologies, implementing strategies to mitigate risks and ensure the continuity of operations.
    Provide regular updates and reporting to the HUB Digitalization & Automation team on ongoing and upcoming projects.

Your background

    A bachelor's or master's degree in engineering, industrial automation, manufacturing, computer science, software engineering or a related field.
    Knowledge of digital technologies, automation systems, and Industry 4.0 concepts. Familiarity with IoT, robotics, data analytics, and integration platforms is essential.
    Proficient with Power Platform.
    Some years of experience as the specialist/lead of digitalization in the manufacturing area and familiar with operation process.
    Strong project management skills to lead the planning, execution, and successful implementation of digital and automation initiatives.
    Strong communication skills to convey complex technological concepts to diverse stakeholders, including non-technical team members and senior management.
    Skills in areas such as manufacturing digitalization, automation and process analysis.
    Strong problem-solving skills to identify challenges in manufacturing processes and develop innovative solutions using digital and automation technologies.
    Proficiency in both spoken and written Finnish and English required.

The question isn't whether you're exposed to market complexity.

The organizations creating the most value from renewable portfolios aren't just optimizing generation.
They're optimizing positions.
Because in today's markets, owning renewable assets is only part of the equation.
Managing the portfolio is where the real differentiation begins.
At what point does a renewable generator stop being just an asset owner and become a market participant?

About the job

About Us

Harmattan AI is a next-generation defense prime building autonomous and scalable defense systems. Following the close of a $200M Series B, valuing the company at $1.4 billion, we are expanding our teams and capabilities to deliver mission-critical systems to allied forces.

Our work is guided by clear values: building technologies with real-world impact, pursuing excellence in everything we do, setting ambitious goals, and taking on the hardest technical challenges. We operate in a demanding environment where rigor, ownership, and execution are expected.

About The Role

The Manufacturing Automation & Digitalization Engineer is a key member of the Automation & Industrialization (A&I) team and a central architect of Harmattan AI's digital manufacturing backbone at its greenfield Wissous facility.

This role coordinates the digitalization of production lines through the definition and integration of MES and ERP architectures, acting as the primary bridge between engineering, production, quality, and supply chain. Working in close partnership with the Odoo Software Engineer, you co-own the digital and operational architecture of the factory from day one.

You capture cross-functional operational needs, define system architecture, and translate requirements into actionable technical specifications — enabling scalable production, tighter quality loops, and full traceability of our autonomous defense systems.

Missions

Cross-Functional Requirements Coordination

    Lead structured operational analysis and requirements workshops with production, engineering, quality, and supply chain — mapping current and target operating modes at line and equipment level, identifying process gaps, and establishing a formal, baselined set of operational needs
    Build and maintain a traceable requirements repository — from stakeholder need to implemented feature — using standard requirements management practices
    Ensure continuous alignment between evolving operational requirements and the deployed system architecture

OT/IT System Architecture & Integration

    Co-define with the Odoo Software Engineer the end-to-end digital architecture of production lines: data flows, system interfaces, API contracts, and OT/IT integration layers
    Define the application landscape: Odoo MRP/MES scope, dedicated MES modules, automated equipment interfaces, IoT/edge connectivity, and data aggregation layer
    Specify connectivity architecture for automated machines and tools: robotic cells, automated test benches, measurement systems, and smart tooling
    Maintain a living architecture reference documenting decisions, interfaces, and data flows

Machine & Tool Connectivity

    Define data acquisition requirements for each automated asset: cycle times, process parameters, alarm states, tool wear indicators, yield and scrap data
    Read and interpret PLC programs, machine data maps, and equipment communication specifications to define precise integration requirements
    Specify edge computing and data normalization layer to aggregate heterogeneous machine data into a unified, ERP-consumable format
    Drive integration of automated test equipment (ATE) and measurement systems into the quality data pipeline — enabling automated pass/fail decisions and SPC data feeds
    Define smart tooling connectivity requirements: torque wrenches, automated dispensing systems, vision inspection systems, and coordinate measurement machines (CMM)
    Oversee machine onboarding process: protocol qualification, data mapping, signal validation, and end-to-end integration testing

Shop-Floor Digitalization

    Define and oversee implementation of digital work instructions and connected operator interfaces, synchronized with machine states and automated process steps

Senior Data Consultant

We are seeking a Senior Specialist – Industry 4.0 & Manufacturing Data to act as a technical authority and strategic advisor across digital manufacturing initiatives. This role blends deep technical expertise, regulated-industry rigor, and client-facing leadership, supporting both delivery excellence and the growth of Deep Digital’s Industry 4.0 services.

Location: Remote - Ireland

Main Responsibilities:

    Act as a trusted senior advisor to clients on Industry 4.0, manufacturing data, and digital transformation
    Lead development of digital transformation strategies and roadmaps, including:
        Manufacturing Data Platforms & Historians
        Unified Namespace (UNS) and event-driven architectures
        OT/IT integration and cloud/hybrid data strategies



    Guide technology selection and architecture across:
        Data integration, streaming, and contextualisation platforms
        MES, LIMS, QMS, CMMS, and Enterprise integrations
        Control systems and OT platforms 
        Advanced analytics, AI/ML, and digital twin use cases
    Embed data, validation, and governance strategies into project designs in collaboration with internal engineering teams and client stakeholders
    Lead small, multidisciplinary teams delivering projects from concept through design, validation, and handover.

Job benefits:

    Hybrid working
    22 days Annual Leave
    5% Pension
    Paid Sick days
    Income Protection
    Life Insurance
    Professional development


Qualiﬁcations:

    Bachelor’s or master's degree in data science, Computer Science, Statistics, or a related ﬁeld and 6+ years of relevant experience. Where a degree is not held—significant professional experience that demonstrates the ability to excel in this role.

    Deep experience/understanding across Industry 4.0 domains, including:
        Control systems, automation, and OT environments. 
        Manufacturing data historians and platforms
        IT/OT integration, networking, and cybersecurity
        Cloud and hybrid infrastructure
        Vertical and horizontal system integration (MES–ERP–Enterprise)



    Strong understanding of:
        Data warehousing concepts, ETL/ELT processes, and data governance models
        ISA-88 and ISA-95 standards for batch and manufacturing operations
        FAIR data principles and data integrity requirements (ALCOA+)
    Solid experience working within GAMP 5 frameworks and Computer Systems Validation (CSV) in regulated manufacturing environments

Preferred Skills:

    Excellent verbal, written, and presentation skills, with the ability to communicate complex concepts to both technical and executive audiences
    Demonstrated ability to manage multiple concurrent projects, prioritise effectively, and meet deadlines
    Strong problem-solving skills with high attention to detail
    Understanding of ontologies and semantic models and their role in data standardisation and interoperability
    Experience with data visualisation or analytics tools (e.g. Power BI, Tableau, Grafana)
    Hands-on experience with SQL, Python, R, or similar data-focused programming languages
    Exposure to PAT, real-time release, predictive analytics, or closed-loop control use cases

About the job

SAEKI - Manufacturing at software speed

Europe's defense industrial base cannot respond at the speed modern conflict requires. Legacy suppliers operate on month-long timelines. Tribal knowledge is retiring. Supply chains are fragile. SAEKI is building the operating system for autonomous factories — the infrastructure that lets Europe manufacture mission-critical components at software speed.

You'll own the quality assurance and metrology infrastructure of Factory ONE — the measurement systems, inspection processes, and quality management framework that give our aerospace and defense customers absolute confidence in every part we ship.

The Role

As the Manufacturing Lead — Quality and Metrology, you are the owner of all quality systems, dimensional inspection, and measurement infrastructure at Factory ONE. Your mandate is to establish AS9100-ready quality management from day one, implement full traceability for defense customers, and build the metrology capabilities that ensure every part meets spec.

Based on-site at Factory ONE in Munich, you will own everything from incoming material inspection to final dimensional verification, establishing the processes and measurement infrastructure that support both CNC and additive manufacturing operations.

What We're Looking For

    7+ years of experience in quality engineering or metrology in aerospace, defense, or high-precision manufacturing environments.
    AS9100 / ISO 9001 Expertise: Deep understanding of aerospace quality management systems. Experience leading or supporting AS9100 certification efforts. Familiarity with NADCAP requirements is a plus.
    Metrology Mastery: Hands-on experience with CMMs (Zeiss, Hexagon, etc.), 3D scanning, surface roughness measurement, and other precision inspection equipment. You can program a CMM and interpret results.
    GD&T Fluency: Expert-level understanding of Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing.
    First Article and Process Qualification: Experience with FAIR (AS9102), PPAP, or equivalent qualification processes for regulated industries.
    Export and Compliance Awareness: Understanding of export compliance requirements (ITAR/EAR awareness) relevant to defense manufacturing.
    Software-Driven Mindset: You see metrology data as a feedback loop for process improvement, not just a gate. You are excited about integrating measurement data into our Factory OS for real-time quality control.
    Location: Based in Munich (on-site at Factory ONE).
    Language: Fluent in German and English.

What We're Not Looking For

    Quality managers who only manage documents and audits.
    People who view quality as a "policing" function. Your systems must accelerate output, not slow it down.
    Metrologists with no interest in additive manufacturing. Our quality challenges span both CNC and AM — you must be curious about both.

What We Offer

    Mission: Rebuild the industrial sovereignty of the West. Your work directly enables the production of mission-critical hardware for defense and space.
    Total Ownership: High-stakes autonomy in your domain. You own both the problems and the solutions — and have the authority to execute.
    The Environment: Access to a 5,000m² state-of-the-art facility featuring 5-axis CNCs, industrial robots, and industrial 3D printers. Your work runs on real hardware from day one.
    The Team: "Special Forces" model. Everyone senior, everyone ships. No politics, no bureaucracy. Direct feedback, high standards.
    Compensation: Competitive salary + meaningful equity.
    Location: Factory ONE in Munich

Apply

    Resume: Focus on systems you've built and owned
    Portfolio: Show us production code you're proud of
    Cover letter: Why this mission? Why now? What would you build first?
