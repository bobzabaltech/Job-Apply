# CV Master Structure 2026

## Purpose

This document defines the structure of the Master CV that will serve as the foundation for all targeted CV versions.

The objective is to create a single source document that represents the complete professional value proposition and can later be adapted for:

* Digital Manufacturing / Manufacturing Systems roles;
* Industrial Data / Manufacturing Intelligence roles;
* MES / Manufacturing Applications roles.

This structure is based on:

* Candidate Positioning Framework 2026;
* Market to Profile Mapping 2026;
* CV Positioning Audit 2026;
* CV Version Strategy 2026;
* Target Roles analysis.

---

# Strategic Objective

The CV must position the candidate as:

> Digital Manufacturing / Manufacturing Systems professional combining deep industrial experience with software engineering and industrial data capabilities.

The CV must avoid being interpreted primarily as:

* Quality Engineer;
* Metrology Specialist;
* Generic Software Engineer;
* Generic Data Engineer.

---

# Master CV Identity

## Recommended Professional Title

Primary:

> Digital Manufacturing & Industrial Data Specialist

Alternative:

> Manufacturing Systems Professional | Industrial Data | Digital Transformation

---

# Document Structure

The Master CV should follow this order:

```text
Professional Identity

↓

Executive Summary

↓

Core Competencies

↓

Professional Experience

↓

Selected Digital Manufacturing Projects

↓

Technical Skills

↓

Education / Certifications

↓

Additional Information
```

---

# 1. Professional Identity

## Objective

The first seconds of recruiter review must establish:

* manufacturing expertise;
* digital capabilities;
* target market category.

---

## Recommended Header

Include:

Name

Professional title:

> Digital Manufacturing & Industrial Data Specialist

Location

Contact information

LinkedIn

Portfolio / GitHub if relevant

---

# 2. Executive Summary

## Purpose

Convert the candidate history into a market-oriented value proposition.

The summary must answer:

1. Who is this professional?
2. What problems does he solve?
3. Why is he different?

---

## Required Elements

### Industrial Foundation

Highlight:

* 22 years manufacturing experience;
* automotive environment;
* production systems;
* quality systems;
* process improvement.

---

### Digital Capability

Highlight:

* software development;
* industrial data;
* analytics;
* digital manufacturing solutions.

---

### Differentiator

The key message:

> Unlike traditional software or data professionals, the candidate understands the industrial processes generating the data and the operational decisions that depend on it.

# 4. Professional Experience

## Objective

Transform experience from task descriptions into business value.

Every position should follow:

```text
Manufacturing Context

↓

Problem / Challenge

↓

Actions and Capabilities

↓

Operational Impact
```

---

# Experience Writing Rules

Avoid:

* long lists of responsibilities;
* isolated tools;
* inspection-focused descriptions.

Prioritize:

* improvements;
* systems;
* decisions;
* measurable outcomes;
* collaboration.

---

## Domain Expertise

* quality systems.

## Digital Potential

* data usage;

* operational analytics.

# Master CV Optimization Rules

## Rule 1

The first impression must be:

> Manufacturing expert with digital capabilities.

Not:

> Software person interested in manufacturing.

---

## Rule 2

Every technical element must connect to industrial value.

Weak:

Strong:

> Python-based tools for industrial data analysis and manufacturing decision support.

# Final CV Test

Before sending any version, verify:

## Question 1

Does a recruiter understand the manufacturing advantage within 10 seconds?

## Question 2

Does the CV contain the keywords searched by target roles?

## Question 3

Do the case studies prove the claimed capabilities?

## Question 4

Does the document position the candidate above traditional quality roles?

If any answer is no, revise before application.
